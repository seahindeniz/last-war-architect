# Last War: Survival Game — the complete master guide

**Last War: Survival Game is a zombie-themed 4X mobile strategy game by FUNFLY PTE. LTD. that has generated over $2 billion in revenue since its August 2023 launch.** Currently in Season 5 ("Wild West") with 1,761+ servers, the game revolves around base-building, hero collection, a rock-paper-scissors troop system (Tank > Missile > Aircraft > Tank), and alliance-driven PvP warfare. This guide synthesizes community consensus from the Last War Handbook, Cpt Hedgehog's Battle HQ, AllClash, Reddit, and dozens of content creators into a single actionable reference covering every game system from HQ 1 through endgame.

---

## Part 1: Early game — rush your HQ above all else (HQ 1–15)

The single most important early-game principle is **rushing your Headquarters level**. Higher HQ unlocks hero level caps (formula: HQ Level × 5 = Max Hero Level), troop tiers, buildings, and dramatically better event rewards. Resource boxes opened at HQ 30 yield far more than the same boxes opened at HQ 15, so hoarding them is optimal.

**Building upgrade priority for HQ 1–15:**

1. **Headquarters** — always the #1 target; every other building exists to enable the next HQ level
2. **Tech Center** — the game's single worst bottleneck; required at nearly every HQ level with the longest timers. Keep it at or 1–2 levels ahead of HQ at all times
3. **Warehouse** — reach level 10 minimum to protect resources from raids. Neglecting this is one of the top beginner mistakes
4. **Resource buildings** — levels 8–10 for Food and Iron production
5. **Hospital** — level 8 minimum; protects troops from permanent death
6. **Wall** — frequently required with high Iron costs; check next 2–3 HQ prerequisites
7. **One Drill Ground** — only upgrade one. Upgrading multiple is a confirmed resource trap
8. **One Combat Center** (Tank, Aircraft, or Missile) — pick your primary troop type early and commit

**Critical early-game rules:** Never let your builder sit idle — queue long builds before sleeping. Only upgrade buildings that are prerequisites for your next HQ level. Skip the Material Workshop upgrade entirely (drains resources for poor returns). Join an active alliance on Day 1 — alliance helps alone save days of building time in the first month.

**Best use of gems/diamonds:** Every diamond spent on **VIP points** provides permanent compound bonuses (resource production, march speed, hero stats, construction speed). This is universally cited as the single best diamond investment at any stage. The 30-day VIP activation costs 10,000 diamonds and is 3× better value than daily activation. Never spend diamonds on instant building completion, random recruitment, or resource packs.

**F2P players earn roughly 6,000–8,000 diamonds per month** through daily tasks (~210/day), world zombie first kills, 3v3 Brawl likes (30/day), Intercity Trade, and events.

### Which free heroes deserve early investment

Focus on exactly **5 core heroes** — spreading upgrades thin across your roster is one of the most damaging beginner mistakes. The community-consensus priority for free/early heroes:

**Murphy** (Tank, Defense, UR) is given free from the tutorial and is the best free tank in the game. He carries early-to-mid game and remains useful in endgame for World Boss and Hero Trials. Skill priority: Ironclad Barrier first (29% physical damage reduction), then Stand Firm (passive 17% damage reduction to front row). Get him to 4-star minimum for the Super Sensory passive.

**Kimberly** (Tank, Attack, UR) is available for roughly $2–5 in the starter offer and is universally considered the single best hero investment in the game. She provides devastating AoE damage and remains relevant from early game through endgame. If you spend anything at all, spend it here.

**Mason** (Tank, SSR→UR) is a strong early damage dealer who forms the best Tank core with Murphy and Kimberly. He can be upgraded to UR at 5-star in Season 1 and continues using cheaper SSR shards even after promotion — a significant economic advantage.

### The 15 mistakes that cripple new accounts

Spending diamonds on instant building completions. Spreading hero upgrades across more than 5 heroes. Playing without an alliance. Ignoring Warehouse upgrades. Neglecting the Drone Center (provides massive hidden power multipliers). Poor stamina usage (save for Zombie Invasion and Alliance Exercise events). Starting PvP too early. Upgrading multiple Drill Grounds. Upgrading the Material Workshop. Over-upgrading resource production buildings early. Not collecting daily free rewards (thousands of diamonds left on the table monthly). Choosing an old, populated server instead of one labeled "New." Using speedups outside of scoring events. Forgetting troop promotion when new tiers unlock (wastes 50% more resources). Letting the Tech Center fall behind HQ level.

## Part 2: Mid-game demands strategic discipline (HQ 15–25)

Around HQ 15, buildings that took hours now take days. This is where strategy matters more than raw activity, and the research tree becomes the primary differentiator between strong and weak accounts. HQ 15 to HQ 25 takes approximately **4–6 weeks** with strategic play.

### Research tree: the path that decides everything

Research priority is the single most consequential mid-game decision. The community-consensus optimal path:

**Development Tree (first priority):** Rush Construction Speed and Research Enhancement nodes immediately — these permanently cut build timers and compound over months. This tree is your economic foundation and should be nearly complete by mid-game. Around HQ 20–25, Development research unlocks extra Barracks (accelerating troop production).

**Alliance Duel Tree (second priority):** Uses Valor Badges (blue hexagonal medals from VS events), not standard resources. This is enormous — it lets you progress without draining iron, food, or oil. It earns VS Day points and unlocks the Special Forces tree faster. Timing strategy: start research before Day 3 (Technology Research Day), use speedups to finish during the scoring window, creating a self-reinforcing cycle.

**Special Forces Tree (available after Tech Center level 20):** This is the direct path to T10 troops. Save ALL Valor Badges for Special Forces. Do not spend them on Intercity Truck, Hero tech, or other trees until your first T10 march is established.

**Economy Tree (parallel):** Fills gaps during downtime. Boost food, metal, and coin production. Doesn't compete with badge-heavy research; provides compound returns over months.

**If running two Tech Centers** (unlocked via research or purchase): Lab 1 handles fast, no-badge trees (Development, Economy). Lab 2 handles badge-heavy trees (Alliance Duel, Special Forces).

### Troop specialization and the formation bonus

Pick your primary troop type early and commit fully. The mono-type formation bonus — **+20% to HP, Attack, and Defense simultaneously** from deploying 5 same-type heroes — is multiplicative and creates a roughly **44% effective power advantage** over mixed formations. This is not a rounding error; it is the single largest free power multiplier in the game.

Tank squads are recommended for new players due to the easiest hero availability (Murphy, Kimberly, Mason are all accessible in the first month). Aircraft squads become dominant around server Day 70+ as they naturally counter the prevalent tank meta. Missile squads serve as a niche counter-pick against aircraft-heavy servers.

### The VS Day system that drives all progression

**Alliance Duel** is a 6-day weekly competitive event that provides 60–80% of endgame progression materials. The daily structure:

- **Monday (Resource Collection):** Radar missions, resource gathering (gold gives most points), drone activities
- **Tuesday (Construction):** Building upgrades, construction speedups — save completed buildings for this day
- **Wednesday (Research):** Technology research, Valor Badge spending
- **Thursday (Hero Progression):** Hero EXP, shards, skill medals, Tavern recruitment — hoard all hero materials for this day
- **Friday (Total Mobilization):** ALL speedup types count; training points have a **4× multiplier** — the most important VS day for point accumulation
- **Saturday (Enemy Buster):** PvP kill day worth **4 victory points** (highest value). Shield up before Friday night sleep

The golden rule: **never use speedups outside of scoring events.** Using speedups randomly wastes half their potential value. This single habit separates fast-progressing accounts from struggling ones.

## Part 3: Endgame power — T10 troops, oil, and the push to HQ 35

### T10 troops are the critical endgame milestone

Unlocking T10 ("Unit X") requires HQ 30, Tech Center 30, Barracks 30, and completion of four final Special Forces research nodes costing **~500M–600M Gold each**. Gold/Coin is the hardest resource to stockpile and the #1 bottleneck.

**Gold acquisition strategies:** Zombie Invasion is the most critical event for T10 progression — strategic play can earn over 1 billion coins per event. Hoard stamina during off weeks, spend ~95% during invasion, buy the 300 and 500 diamond stamina packs on event days, participate in every alliance rally, and prioritize boss-level zombies. Dead base farming — scouting inactive players holding 500M–1B total resources and attacking up to 5 times daily — is a legitimate late-game strategy. Keep Gold Refineries maxed and boosted, and when getting resource choice chests, **manually select coin** (auto-select may choose food or iron).

### T11 troops represent the current power ceiling

Introduced in Season 5 via the **Armament Institute**, T11 offers a 5–10% stat boost over T10. Two T11 types exist: **Armored Trooper** (reduces attribute penalties with insufficient units — best for defense) and **Assault Raider** (reduces severe wound rate — best for attacking). You can swap types every 72 hours. The unlock requires completing 4 sequential Armament Research pieces, with the final unlock costing 120K materials + 200 cores + 1.2M oil. Default to Assault Raiders for general play; switch to Armored Troopers for defensive operations.

### Age of Oil (HQ 31–35)

After Season 2, Oil becomes a mandatory new resource and THE main bottleneck for HQ 31+ progression. Total Oil needed for HQ 31–35 building upgrades is approximately **12.6 million**. You can unlock up to 5 oil wells (2 from Special Forces research, 3 from HQ leveling). Rush to HQ 31 immediately after Age of Oil arrives — the sooner you unlock oil wells, the better your long-term trajectory.

### What separates good endgame players from great ones

**Event mastery** (strategic vs. casual participation creates a 3–5× advancement difference), **resource timing** (knowing when to hoard, when to spend — particularly gold management for T10/T11 research), **concentration of power** (one maxed squad beats three mediocre squads), **research path discipline** (never wasting Valor Badges on non-essential trees), **VS Day optimization** (timing completions and speedups for maximum scoring), and **understanding combat math** (using type advantages, morale, and formation bonuses to punch above their power weight).

## Part 4: The hero system — the game's most critical mechanic

### Complete hero tier list by squad type

**Tank Squad (SS/S tier):** Kimberly (Attack, UR) is the premier AoE damage dealer and #1 overall hero across virtually every community tier list. Murphy (Defense, UR) is the best free tank, given on Day 1. Williams (Defense, UR) provides ultimate team-wide damage reduction. Marshall (Support, UR) is the **only UR support hero in the game** and is irreplaceable — he boosts every squad type. Stetmann (Attack, UR) adds significant energy damage to multiple opponents.

**Tank Squad (A/B tier):** Mason (Attack, SSR→UR) is the best SSR tank, extremely accessible. Scarlett (Defense, SSR→UR) is a decent budget frontliner until your full UR roster is built. Richard (Attack, SSR) struggles to hold frontline. Kane and Gump are placeholder-only.

**Aircraft Squad (SS/S tier):** DVA (Attack, UR) has the highest burst damage in the game — her 3rd skill hits ALL enemies. Carlie (Defense, UR) is the ONLY Aircraft-class tank and is mandatory for any Aircraft lineup.

**Aircraft Squad (S/A tier):** Morrison (Attack, UR) shreds enemy defense by up to 25%. Lucius (Defense, UR) becomes arguably the best tank in the game once his exclusive weapon unlocks. Schuyler (Attack, UR) disrupts enemy formations with stun mechanics.

**Missile Squad (SS/S tier):** Tesla (Attack, UR) is the premier missile damage dealer — Electric Grid Lockdown hits 3 back-row units, bypassing tanks entirely. Fiona (Attack, UR) has the highest raw damage output among missile heroes.

**Missile Squad (A tier):** Swift (Attack, UR) is strong early but falls off sharply — the most commonly overrated hero. Adam (Defense, UR) is the primary missile tank. McGregor (Defense, UR) has a taunt mechanic that can get him killed without heavy investment.

### Skill prioritization follows a universal pattern

**For Defenders (tanks):** Tactic (Top Right) → Passive (Bottom Left) → Auto-Attack (Top Left). Their job is to survive; offensive skills are lowest priority.

**For Attackers (DPS):** Tactic (Top Right) → Auto-Attack (Top Left) → Passive (Bottom Left). Maximize damage output.

**For Marshall (support):** Top Right (attack buff) → Bottom Left (passive) → Top Left.

Within a squad, the community-consensus medal investment order is:

- **Tank:** Kimberly > Murphy > Marshall > Stetmann > Williams
- **Aircraft:** DVA > Carlie > Morrison > Lucius > Schuyler
- **Missile:** Tesla > Fiona > Swift > Adam > McGregor

Upgrade ALL skills to Level 10 first (cost-effective baseline), then push in increments of 10 levels. Complete one hero's essential skills fully before starting another.

### Star upgrades and the 4-star power spike

Each hero can reach 5 stars, requiring a total of **975 shards per hero**. The critical milestone is **4-star**, which unlocks **Super Sensory** — a passive granting +20% HP/ATK/DEF plus 10% skill cooldown speed. This represents the single largest individual hero power spike.

Star upgrade priority order mirrors the tier list: Kimberly → Murphy → Williams → Marshall → Stetmann (Tank); DVA → Carlie → Lucius → Morrison → Schuyler (Aircraft); Tesla → Swift/Adam → Fiona → McGregor (Missile).

Key shard strategy: Focus universal UR shards on heroes whose shards are NOT in selection chests (Adam, Fiona, Morrison). Season 3+ Hero Exchange Vouchers allow swapping levels AND star rank 1:1 — ideal swaps include Mason→any UR (Mason rebuilds cheaply with SSR shards).

### Best formations by squad type

**Tank (meta early/mid):** Murphy (Front) + Williams (Front) | Kimberly (Back) + Stetmann (Back) + Marshall (Back). Budget: Replace Stetmann with Mason if unavailable.

**Aircraft (meta mid/late):** Carlie (Front) + Lucius (Front) | DVA (Back) + Morrison (Back) + Schuyler (Back). Advanced PvP variant: Murphy replacing Carlie in front row — his Exclusive Weapon makes him tankier despite losing 5% type bonus.

**Missile (counter-pick):** McGregor (Front) + Adam (Front) | Tesla (Back) + Fiona (Back) + Swift (Back). Venom's UR promotion in Season 5 makes 5-hero Missile significantly more viable.

**Front row = ALWAYS Defense heroes. Back row = ALWAYS Attack heroes + Support.** Placing damage dealers in the front row gets them killed in seconds; defense heroes in the back row waste their potential.

## Part 5: Gear crafting demands ruthless focus

### The 10-level increment rule

Gear levels go from 1 to 40. **Every 10 levels (Lv 10, 20, 30, 40), gear unlocks a new bonus attribute** — a secondary stat providing major power increases only gained at these milestones. The optimal strategy is upgrading gear in 10-level blocks: push one piece to 10 before starting another, then push to 20, then 30, then 40. Spreading upgrades thin across many pieces is a trap.

Only **Legendary (Gold/UR) gear** deserves long-term investment. It is the only rarity that can be star-promoted (leading to Mythic/Red). Skip green and blue; use purple temporarily as placeholders; save all high-value materials (Dielectric Ceramics, blueprints, Upgrade Ore) for Legendary gear.

### Star promotion: the years-long project

After reaching Level 40, Legendary gear enters the star promotion system. Per gear piece: 1-star costs 5 Legendary Blueprints, 2-star costs 10, 3-star costs 15, 4-star costs 20, and the final 5-star (transforming gear to Mythic) costs 10 Mythic Blueprints. **Total per piece: 50 Legendary + 10 Mythic Blueprints. Total per hero (4 pieces): 200 Legendary + 40 Mythic Blueprints.** One piece to 5-star takes 6–18 months; one hero's full set is a 2–3 year project.

**Blueprint acquisition:** Spend Honor Points ONLY on Legendary Blueprints — this is the #1 gear mistake to avoid. Bullet Events and Ammo Bonanza are major blueprint sources. Mythic Blueprints come primarily from Ammo Bonanza, Season Store, and anniversary events.

### Gear priority by role

**Front Row Tanks (Murphy, Williams, Carlie, Lucius, Adam, McGregor):** Armor → Radar → optional Gun. Primary stats: HP, Defense, Damage Reduction.

**Back Row DPS (Kimberly, DVA, Tesla, Morrison, Fiona, Stetmann):** Gun → Chip → Radar. Primary stats: Attack, Crit, Skill Damage.

**Support (Marshall):** Radar → Armor (survivability priority — dead support provides zero buffs).

The universal endgame wisdom: **eventually put Radar on everyone.** It reduces incoming skill damage universally and works equally for PvP and PvE.

### Crafting pipeline

The full chain: 256 Screws → 64 Refined Iron Modules → 16 Super Alloys → 4 Synthetic Resins → 1 Dielectric Ceramic (the key Legendary crafting material). Material Workshop produces Screws; dismantling lower-tier gear returns crafting components. Complete full Legendary gear sets for your **3 core heroes first**, then expand. Never spread gear across 10+ heroes — none will reach competitive levels.

## Part 6: Spending smart — from F2P to whale

### The must-buy purchases (Tier 1)

The **Kimberly Starter Pack** (~$2–5, one-time) unlocks the game's strongest hero and pays off immediately. The **2nd and 3rd Builder unlocks** (one-time purchases) permanently allow simultaneous building upgrades, saving hundreds of hours. The **2nd Tech Center unlock** doubles research throughput — critical once research times hit days. The **Monthly Pass** (~$5–10/month) is consistently rated the game's best recurring deal, with value exceeding 8,200% per community analysis (daily diamonds, stamina, speed-ups, extra squad slot). The **Growth Fund** (one-time) unlocks up to **50,000 diamonds** progressively as you level HQ — the smartest long-term investment for anyone planning to play seriously.

### VIP breakpoints worth targeting

VIP level is permanent; VIP status requires diamond activation. The 30-day activation at 10,000 diamonds is always 3× better value than daily activation.

- **VIP 4:** Auto-help unlocked (major quality-of-life)
- **VIP 6:** Second builder (essential for F2P)
- **VIP 8:** Unlocks exclusive Agent Shirley, third march queue, daily shards, campaign auto-clear — this is **the competitive threshold**, achievable by F2P in approximately 8 months
- **VIP 12:** Mega Mode (Secret Mobile Squad automation), fourth march — a genuine game-changer but requires whale-level investment (~500,000 points, roughly 7 years F2P)
- **VIP 13+:** Diminishing returns begin significantly

### F2P optimization strategy

F2P players can absolutely compete but must be ruthlessly efficient. Diamond spending priority: VIP points always → 30-day VIP activation once at VIP 3+ → shields during Enemy Buster/Capitol War → VIP Store stamina (300 diamonds/week, best value in the entire shop). Never buy instant building completions, resource packs, gear materials, Hero EXP, or 5-minute speedups with diamonds. The 5-minute speedups are **60% more expensive per hour** than 8-hour ones.

### Light spender sweet spot ($5–50/month)

Purchase order: Monthly Pass → Kimberly Pack → Permanent Builders → 2nd Tech Center → Growth Fund → Daily Sale Bundles (only when targeting specific UR hero shards) → Season Battle Pass. This combination provides overwhelming advantage over pure F2P at minimal cost.

## Part 7: Events — where 60–80% of progression materials come from

### Alliance Duel is the single most important event

The 6-day weekly cycle (Resource Collection → Construction → Research → Hero → Total Mobilization → Enemy Buster) drives all serious progression. Day 5 (Friday, Total Mobilization) and Day 6 (Saturday, Enemy Buster at 4 victory points) are worth the most points. Alliance Duel Research is critical — it increases points earned by up to 150% and unlocks better reward chests.

The "waterfall training" technique maximizes Friday scoring: train low-tier troops → upgrade them through barracks levels. Each upgrade counts for 4× points, creating enormous scoring efficiency.

### Kill Event survival guide (Enemy Buster, Saturday)

Points come from killing enemy troops, not just attacking. Rival alliance member kills award **500 points (5× multiplier)** versus 100 for standard kills — always prioritize rival targets. Use Alliance Assaults for free teleportation to the rival's warzone. Never attack solo; teleport with 5+ allies and garrison each other. Non-combatants should activate 24-hour shields before Friday night sleep. Never send troops to gather on Day 6 — they will be destroyed, gifting free points to enemies. Remove troops from wall defenses to minimize losses per breach.

### Cross-Server War (Warzone Duel)

A bracket-tournament system where entire servers clash. The attacking server (determined by weekly preparation scores) must capture the enemy Capitol and push occupation to 100% — if they fail, defenders win by default. The Capitol sits within a Contaminated Zone where march speed drops drastically and shields cannot be activated. Every power level has a role: top alliances control the Capitol, mid-power alliances control the 4 surrounding Cannons (their continuous fire shifts the battle), growing alliances act as burn squads and scouts. Cannon control often determines the outcome more than raw Capitol assault power.

### Ghost Ops is irreplaceable

Running weekly after Season 1, Ghost Ops is the **ONLY source** of fragments for late-game heroes including Lucius, Morrison, Adam, and Fiona. Missing Ghost Ops weeks means permanently falling behind on hero development. Thursday Ghost Ops should be treated as a mandatory appointment.

### Desert Storm — the weekly alliance battlefield

Desert Storm is a weekly PvP event where two alliances from different servers fight for control over 11 buildings on a desert map. The side that accumulates the most points wins. It is one of the game's most rewarding events, providing Honor Points, resources, and decorations.

**Participation requirements:** The alliance must be in the server's top 32 and at least 7 days old. Individual players need HQ level 15+ and must not be on polluted land.

**Team composition:** Each team has 20 starters + 10 substitutes. A single alliance can register two teams (A and B), allowing up to 40 starters and 20 substitutes total. Substitutes can only enter when someone leaves, and once a player leaves they cannot return — coordinate substitute rotations in advance.

**Weekly schedule:** Registration opens Monday and closes Wednesday. R4/R5 officers select the event time from three options. Matchmaking occurs Thursday. The battle takes place Friday.

**Key battlefield rules:** Players receive maximum troops for their level — units do not permanently die and are fully restored after the battle. All buffs apply (technologies, VIP, skins, titles, decorations). Free teleport recharges every 2 minutes (reducible via building capture). Healing speed-ups used during the event are NOT refunded.

**Battlefield layout — 11 buildings in three zones:**

**Side buildings (L1–L4 and R1–R4) — available from battle start:**

- **Oil Refinery (L2, R3)** — the primary point source among side buildings. 4,500 alliance points for first capture, 50/second for occupation. These are the highest-value early targets.
- **Field Hospital (L3–L4, R1–R2)** — 2,700 alliance points for first capture, 30/second for occupation. Occupation bonus: restores 15 units every 10 seconds. Capturing all four hospitals early gives a massive sustain advantage.
- **Info Centre (L1)** — 900 alliance points for first capture, 10/second. Occupation bonus: reduces building capture time by 50%.
- **Science Hub (R4)** — 900 alliance points for first capture, 10/second. Occupation bonus: reduces teleport cooldown by 50% (2 minutes → 1 minute). This is a critical strategic building — faster teleports mean faster repositioning across the entire map.

**Central buildings (C1–C3) — shield drops after 10 minutes:**

- **Nuclear Silo (C2)** — the single highest-value building. 6,750 alliance points for first capture, 75/second for occupation. Controlling the Silo in the final minutes is often the deciding factor.
- **Arsenal (C1)** — 900 points for first capture, 10/second. Occupation bonus: +15% Attack, Defense, and HP for all your heroes.
- **Mercenary Factory (C3)** — 900 points for first capture, 10/second. Occupation bonus: -15% Attack, Defense, and HP for enemy heroes. Combined with Arsenal, these two swing combat effectiveness by 30%.

**Additional map elements:**

- **Points Supply Boxes:** Buildings accumulate excess points over time (shown above the building). When a building changes hands, those accumulated points scatter across the battlefield as collectible supply boxes — send scouts to grab them.
- **Oil Wells:** Appear near the end of the battle. 4 alliance points/second for gathering. Only worthwhile if the match outcome is already decided; diverting troops to gather during a close match is a mistake.

**Battle phases and recommended tactics:**

The battle lasts approximately 30–40 minutes and unfolds in three stages. **Stage 1 (0–10 min):** Capture side buildings — prioritize Oil Refineries, Hospitals, and Science Hub. **Stage 2 (10+ min):** Central shield drops — contest Nuclear Silo, Arsenal, and Mercenary Factory. **Stage 3 (late game):** Oil Wells appear; Supply Boxes become collectible from contested buildings.

**Recommended team structure (5 teams of 4):** Teams T1–T4 each target specific buildings (nearest 2 Hospitals, nearest Oil Refinery, Science Hub). Team T5 acts as a flex/support squad — reinforcing contested points, capturing enemy-side buildings, or pivoting to central objectives after the shield drops. The leader should call targets clearly ("T1, go to R4") using voice chat or a messaging app.

**Critical strategic tips:**

- **Don't rush the same building as everyone else** — spreading out is far more effective than stacking.
- **Nuclear Silo timing trick:** Let the enemy hold the Silo initially, then capture it in the final minutes to harvest maximum accumulated points while denying them the long-term bonus.
- **Ghost marches:** Send single-troop marches to bait attacks or confuse enemies about your real movements.
- **After capturing a building**, leave 1 hero inside to hold it and move the rest to the next objective immediately.
- **Science Hub is a force multiplier** — halving teleport cooldown lets your entire team reposition twice as fast.
- **Always reinforce buildings under siege** — losing a held building costs both occupation points and scatters your accumulated points for the enemy to collect.
- **Matchmaking manipulation:** You can attempt to lower your team's visible Power before Thursday matchmaking by temporarily removing hero gear and drone components on Wednesday evening.
- **Do not register players who cannot attend** — empty slots inflate your matchmaking Power without contributing combat strength.

**Rewards:** Personal rewards scale with individual score (maxing out at 200,000 points) and include resources, Honor Points, and decorations. Alliance rewards are chests distributed to all members based on victory/defeat outcome. Winners receive significantly more prizes — every participant's contribution matters.

### Season structure and migration

Every season follows an **8-week structure** with progressive content unlocks. Season buildings and seasonal resources (Coal, Mithril, Coffee) do not carry over — max seasonal buildings for profession XP before they disappear. Core progression (HQ level, heroes, troops, research, gear) carries forward permanently.

Season differences are substantial: Season 1 (Crimson Plague) introduces virus resistance mechanics, Season 2 (Polar Storm) adds temperature management with NO inter-server wars (local only), Season 3 (Golden Kingdom) features curse resistance and desert mechanics, Season 4 (Evernight Isle) introduces Tactics Cards, and Season 5 (Wild West) adds the Coffee/CrystalGold economy and Caffeine Institute.

**Transfer Surge** occurs during the Season Celebration period (after each season's Week 8), allowing server migration between warzones of similar age. All progress is preserved. Choose servers matching your goals and check server age, activity level, and dominant alliance politics before transferring.

## Part 8: Alliance politics — where wars are won before they're fought

### The NAP system holds servers together

Non-Aggression Pacts (NAPs) are diplomatic agreements between alliances to avoid attacking each other. NAP10 protects the top 10 alliances, NAP15 the top 15, NAP20 the top 20 — determined by server population and activity. Standard NAP rules: no attacking members' bases, no looting commercial trucks or secret tasks, no scouting. Farm alliances (same tag as main alliance) are typically protected. Violations escalate from warnings to mediation to Kill-on-Sight status.

**The strongest servers don't eliminate competition — they organize it.** Stability attracts high spenders who hate wasting resources on internal drama. An R4 diplomat with strong people skills managing inter-alliance relationships is one of the highest-value roles in any alliance.

### Rally mechanics in detail

Only the Rally Leader's hero stats, research, buffs, and debuffs influence rally combat calculations. R4/R5 officer-initiated rallies provide a **2.5–5% damage bonus** to the entire rally group. Rally prep times range from 5 to 60 minutes. Auto-rally should always be enabled to help fill rallies automatically. The optimal personal strategy: send your strongest squad to join others' rallies while using a weaker squad to lead your own.

### Capitol capture and the President system

City progression opens Level 1 cities on Day 4, scaling to the Capitol (City of Apocalypse) on Day 28. Only R4/R5 can declare war on cities, limited to 2 declarations per day. The winning alliance leader becomes **President** with authority to appoint officials — government titles grant powerful server-wide boosts to construction, research, resources, and military strength. Must own adjacent Military Strongholds with connected territory to declare war on higher-level cities.

## Part 9: Combat math — why a 500K player can beat a 1.5M player

### The counter system creates massive swings

Tank vs. Missile Vehicle: Tanks deal **+20% damage** to Missiles AND take **-20% damage** from Missiles, creating a **40% effective power swing**. In practice, 100K Tank troops facing 100K Missile troops results in the Tank army winning with approximately 50% surviving. Multiply this by the 20% formation bonus from same-type heroes, and the math becomes overwhelming: a well-composed mono-type army with type advantage fights at roughly **3.6× effective power** compared to a mixed squad with type disadvantage.

### Morale is the most underutilized mechanic

Each 1% morale advantage translates to 1% extra damage, up to a 200% boost. Morale drops as you lose troops, creating snowball effects. Sources include War Fever, territory control, equipment, alliance buffs, hero skills, and Special Forces research. The War Leader profession's Inspire skill adds +10%. Most players ignore morale entirely, yet it routinely determines the outcome of close battles.

### Hospital management prevents catastrophic losses

Injured troops fill the Hospital up to capacity; overflow troops are **permanently lost**. Target hospital capacity of 50,000+ for regular PvP and 100,000+ for server wars. The Emergency Center (unlocking at HQ 22) saves a percentage of overflow as "fainted units" — upgrade it aggressively. The most powerful free healing method: **heal in small batches (50–200 troops), request alliance help, and repeat.** With 30 active alliance members, you can instant-heal batches worth ~2 hours of normal healing time.

Having at least one hero on your wall — even a weak one — reduces troop losses per breach by **40–50%** compared to an empty wall. Counterintuitively, removing all wall defenses while keeping a hero present can also reduce losses per breach attack.

## Part 10: Decorations, base skins, and hidden power multipliers

### Decoration tier list: damage reduction dominates

Decorations provide **passive, account-wide stat buffs** that apply to ALL heroes in ALL modes without activation. Only invest in UR (Gold/Orange) decorations — SR and SSR are resource sinks.

**S-Tier (Damage Reduction):** Win in 2025, Win in 2026, Torch Relay, Rock the End, Joyful Bunny. Damage Reduction is currently the strongest PvP stat because it scales multiplicatively rather than additively.

**A+ Tier (Skill Damage / March Size):** Easter Egg-sassin, Lovely Bears, Fabulous Phonograph, Win in 2024, Turkey Swashbuckler, Cornucopia, and critically the **God of Judgment** — the ONLY decoration providing +10 March Size plus HP/ATK/DEF.

**A-Tier (Critical Damage):** Tower of Victory, Cheese Manor, Rosy Cabriolet, Golden Marshal, Colorful Christmas Tree.

Upgrade path: Get ALL UR decorations to **Level 3 first** (unlocks special bonus milestone), then push S-Tier to Level 4+, then A+/A Tier. The **Eternal Pyramid** provides up to +55% construction speed at Level 7 — essential for progression, especially before Season 3.

### Base skins provide real combat bonuses

Base skins grant permanent passive buffs: typical bonuses include +5% Attack, +5% Defense, +5% HP. The Dual Propeller Base, Mechanical T-Rex, and Eternal Frost Castle provide some of the strongest combat bonuses. Base skins offer both "equipped" bonuses (active when worn) and "hold" bonuses (passive just for owning them). Participate in seasonal Monopoly-style events to acquire the best skins.

## Part 11: Items, resources, and the hoarding philosophy

### Items to hoard — never use randomly

**Resource Chests** scale with HQ level and are protected from raids while in inventory — save them for when you actually need resources for a specific upgrade. **Speed-ups** should only be spent during scoring events (Arms Race, Alliance Duel). **Hero Return Recruitment Tickets** should be saved between seasons for seasonal resource drops on Day 1. **Valor Badges** should be reserved exclusively for Special Forces research (the T10 path). **Skill Medals** become a major bottleneck from Season 2 onward. **Legendary/Mythical Blueprints** are irreplaceable — target them from Glittering Market and Total Mobilization. **Decorate your Dreams spins** persist between events; save to 20 for guaranteed milestone rewards.

### Stamina management maximizes event returns

Natural regeneration provides 1 point per 5 minutes (288/day), plus two daily 50-stamina bonus claims with a 4-hour cooldown (total ~388/day without purchases). The VIP Store offers 250 stamina for 300 diamonds — the best gem-to-stamina ratio available and a mandatory weekly purchase. Save stamina for 2–3 weeks before each new season starts. During Zombie Invasion events, deploy ~95% of stored stamina for maximum coin yield.

### The daily checklist that compounds over months

Secret tasks (Yours/Assist/Loot), truck sends and plunders, 2 free stamina claims, 3v3 Brawl likes (30 diamonds), 5 arena wins, 3 Campaign battlefields, hero and survivor recruitment, armored truck missions, VIP/Mall/Hot Deals free loot chests, at least 1 walker kill, train rides, radar tasks, resource farming from inactive players, and checking Arms Race phase rotations. Consistent 15–20 minute daily routines across 3–4 check-ins beat marathon sessions. Quality compounds; inconsistency kills accounts.

## Part 12: Hidden mechanics the game never explains

**Survivor switching trick:** Move your best survivors between buildings to apply bonuses multiple times — start research in Building 1 with best survivors, then move them to Building 2 before starting another research.

**Gathering spot waypoints:** Gathering spots dramatically reduce march times to distant targets during wars and don't alert enemies with notifications.

**Morale snowball:** Because morale drops as you lose troops, early-battle advantages compound rapidly. The player who wins the first exchange often wins the entire battle by an increasingly large margin.

**The formation bonus exceeds individual hero quality:** It is better to use a lower-tier hero of the correct type than a higher-tier hero of the wrong type. The 20% formation bonus typically outweighs individual hero power differences — yet the game never explains this clearly.

**Damage Reduction vs. flat HP:** Damage Reduction scales multiplicatively in PvP, making it more efficient per stat point than flat HP at higher levels. This is why Damage Reduction decorations sit in S-tier while HP decorations rank lower.

**Season 5 hidden mechanics:** Engineer profession reduces building costs for ALL seasonal buildings (reset profession to Engineer before building). Doom Walker first-kill rewards only count after the official season update notification, not at server reset. High Noon alt account dueling provides 10,000–15,000 guaranteed CrystalGold daily and is a legitimate game mechanic.

## Part 13: The Drone system — hidden power multiplier most players neglect

The Tactical Drone is one of the game's most impactful systems yet one of the least understood. It continuously attacks enemies in battle while remaining invulnerable, and its stat bonuses transfer directly to your heroes at a conversion ratio. Proper drone upgrading via leveling, components, Combat Boost, and Skill Chips compounds into a 60–150% combat power increase when fully optimized.

### Drone Center basics

The Drone Center unlocks after clearing Block 38 (which also rescues Monica). The building itself cannot be upgraded but provides access to four subsystems: Data Training (leveling), Components (gear), Skill Chips (abilities), and Skins (cosmetic with minor stat bonuses). At drone level 15, components unlock. At levels 31, 51, 71, 91, and 111, the drone's skill level increases and its appearance changes. At level 150, the drone transforms into a B-2 Stealth Bomber. After level 150, every level requires Drone Parts (not just every 5th level). After level 250, further Tech Research is needed to unlock more levels.

**Drone leveling** requires Battle Data (the XP fuel) and Drone Parts (the bottleneck material). Battle Data comes from farming Doom Elites (~1,600 per level-20 elite), Campaign Store exchanges (1,000 points = 10,000 data), Radar Tasks, and the Operation Phoenix Helicopter. Drone Parts come from daily tasks, VIP Store, Alliance Store, Campaign Store, events, and Radar missions. Training has a chance for critical hits (bonus progress), but this cannot occur when progress is 80%+ or when Drone Parts are consumed. Upgrade your drone on Monday (VS Resource Collection day) for Alliance Duel points.

### Drone Components (gear for your drone)

Six component slots unlock at drone level 15, divided into two groups. Left side (Radar, Engine, Armor) stacks defense; right side (Missile, Fuel Cell, Thermal Imager) stacks offense. Left-side components drop roughly twice as often as right-side ones from random chests — this creates an imbalance where your defense skyrockets while attack lags behind. The fix: whenever you get a Component Choice Chest, always pick a right-side piece (Missile, Fuel, or Thermal).

**Upgrading components** works in two phases. Until Level 7, three identical components of the same type and level merge into one higher-level component. After Level 7, components switch to an EXP system where you feed duplicates directly. Open component chests and merge on Wednesday (VS Research Day) for Alliance Duel points.

### Combat Boost — the centralized upgrade system

Combat Boost is the unified progression system that replaced individual chip-level upgrades. It provides global boosts to Drone HP, Drone Attack, and Drone Defense, and these bonuses apply to ALL equipped Skill Chips simultaneously. The system unlocks on server Day 82.

**How to level Combat Boost:** Feed it Drone System EXP Cards (from Alliance Store, Skill Chip Chests), drone materials, and — critically — unused Skill Chips. Common, Rare, and Epic chips you don't use should be fed directly into Combat Boost rather than hoarded. When replacing SSR chips with UR chips, disintegrate the old SSR chips and use them as Combat Boost fuel.

**Key Combat Boost milestones:**

- **Level 10:** Skill Chip slots unlock (4 slots: Initial Move, Defense, Attack, Interference) and first chip preset becomes active
- **Level 150:** 2nd chip preset slot unlocks — allows a separate chip loadout for a second squad
- **Level 300:** 3rd chip preset slot unlocks — now you can customize three squads independently
- **Level 450:** 4th chip preset slot unlocks — full flexibility across all squads
- **Levels 150, 300, 450, 600, 750:** Skill Chip bonus level increases by 1 each time (star-based buffs on individual chips)

The preset system is enormously powerful because it lets you tailor chip sets for different squads — one loadout for tanks, another for aircraft, and swap without losing progress. Rushing to Level 150 is the first priority; Level 300 is the competitive threshold.

### The Chip Lab — crafting your own chips

The Chip Lab is a building introduced in October 2024. It unlocks at HQ level 5 (basic) and can be upgraded to level 35 (with levels 31–35 requiring Age of Oil after Season 2). However, all important chip crafting unlocks by level 20:

- **Chip Lab Level 15:** Unlocks Legendary Initial Move Chip crafting
- **Chip Lab Level 20:** Unlocks ALL remaining Legendary Chip crafting
- **Levels 21–35:** No new chip unlocks — deprioritize these upgrades in favor of Drill Ground, Tech Center, or Barracks

The Chip Lab lets you craft chips (selecting Tank, Aircraft, or Missile Vehicle type), dismantle unwanted chips for resources, and reset chip levels to recover materials. Crafting materials come from VS battles, chip chests, and Alliance Duel rewards (3rd, 6th, and 9th boxes). Crafting speed-ups are the same as equipment crafting speed-ups and do NOT count toward Alliance Duel or Personal Armament points.

### Skill Chip types — the four categories

Every chip preset has four slots, one for each category:

**Initial Move Chip (shield icon):** Activates at the start of battle. Creates a damage-absorbing shield for your front-row heroes for several seconds. Ideal for tank formations. The Legendary version (Absolute Quantum Field) additionally provides a global damage reduction buff that persists for the rest of the battle after the shield expires.

**Defense Chip (shield with cross):** Boosts defense stats. The Legendary version (Gravitational Resonance Armor) provides a type-specific defense buff that lasts the entire battle, plus a front-row defense boost. This is the longest-lasting defensive buff in the chip system.

**Attack Chip (sword icon):** Triggers each time the drone bombs. Boosts attack power of your heroes. The Legendary version (Enhanced Firepower) increases attack and adds extra drone bombing targets. At star level 5, drone targets increase to 3 — a major power spike.

**Interference Chip (lightning icon):** Extends damage duration and intensity of drone attacks. The Legendary version (Memory Ultra Fission) boosts back-row heroes' damage while simultaneously reducing enemy Tactic damage. This is the chip with the highest overall impact and should be crafted first.

### Epic vs. Legendary (UR) chips — the critical difference

This is one of the most common mistakes: players invest heavily in Epic (purple/SSR) chips and resist replacing them. A fresh Legendary chip outperforms an invested Epic chip every time because of how their effects work:

- **Epic chip effects are temporary** — typically lasting 15 seconds, which expires before most battles are halfway done
- **Legendary chip effects last until battle ends** — providing sustained benefits throughout the entire fight
- **Epic chips have single-target bonuses** (e.g., front-row only OR back-row only); Legendary chips provide BOTH a type-specific bonus AND a global squad bonus simultaneously

**SSR to UR conversion benchmarks:** An 8-star SSR chip roughly equals a 3-star UR chip. A 10-star SSR chip roughly equals a 4-star UR chip. This means you should transition to UR chips as soon as possible rather than continuing to invest in SSR chips.

Each Epic and Legendary chip comes in three type variants: Tank, Aircraft, and Missile Vehicle. The chip's type must match your hero formation type for maximum benefit — a Tank chip on an Aircraft squad wastes the type-specific bonus. This reinforces the mono-type formation principle.

### The four Legendary (UR) chips — names and crafting priority

**1. Memory Ultra Fission (Interference — craft FIRST):** When the drone bombs, your type-matched heroes gain a damage increase and enemy Tactic damage is reduced. This is the single most impactful chip across all game modes. Craft it matched to your main squad's type (Tank/Aircraft/Missile). Cost: 800 Premium Chip Material.

**2. Gravitational Resonance Armor (Defense — craft SECOND):** At battle start, your type-matched heroes gain a defense boost; front-row heroes gain an additional defense boost lasting the entire battle. The longest-duration defensive chip. Cost: 800 Premium Chip Material.

**3. Absolute Quantum Field (Initial Move — craft THIRD):** Creates a shield at battle start for front-row heroes, then provides a global damage reduction buff for the rest of the battle. Excellent for tank formations. Cost: 800 Premium Chip Material (note: the Epic Initial Move chip costs 800 Basic Chip Material — double the normal 400 for other Epic chips, reflecting its high value).

**4. Enhanced Firepower (Attack — craft FOURTH):** When the drone bombs, heroes gain an attack boost and the drone hits additional targets. Becomes significantly better at star level 5 (3 drone targets). Cost: 800 Premium Chip Material.

### Star upgrade priority for chips

The boost from star level 1 to star level 2 is significantly larger than subsequent upgrades. Therefore: get ALL four Legendary chips to 2-star first before pushing any single chip higher. After that, follow this order:

**Star levels 1–5:** Memory Ultra Fission → Gravitational Resonance Armor → Enhanced Firepower → Absolute Quantum Field. (Attack chip is prioritized over Initial Move because star levels 2 and 5 add extra drone targets.)

**Star levels 5+:** Memory Ultra Fission → Gravitational Resonance Armor → Absolute Quantum Field → Enhanced Firepower. (After star 5, Attack chip's incremental gains diminish.)

Keep chip levels uniform within a squad — if one reaches star 2, bring all others to star 2 before advancing further. Prioritize your main squad completely before investing in secondary squads.

### Optimal chip configurations by squad type

**Tank Squad:** Gravitational Resonance Armor (Defense) + Absolute Quantum Field (Initial Move) form the defensive core. Memory Ultra Fission (Tank variant) provides sustained damage. Enhanced Firepower adds multi-target pressure. This is the current meta-dominant configuration for its survivability in Alliance Wars and CSW.

**Aircraft Squad:** Memory Ultra Fission (Aircraft variant) + Enhanced Firepower prioritize burst damage. Gravitational Resonance Armor provides survivability. This configuration excels at fast combat resolution and PvE clearing.

**Missile Squad:** Memory Ultra Fission (Missile variant) + Gravitational Resonance Armor form the balanced core. Absolute Quantum Field provides initial protection. This serves as an effective counter-meta build.

### Skill Chip Chests — acquisition sources

**Rare Skill Chip Chests:** Up to 5 weekly from Alliance Store (Alliance points). Contains Epic chips and basic materials.

**Epic Skill Chip Chests:** 1 weekly from Campaign Store. Contains Epic/Legendary chips with better odds plus 100 Premium Chip Material per chest.

**Legendary Skill Chip Chests:** Available monthly from VIP Store and special events. Contains only Legendary chips (8.33% chance per type variant) plus 100 Premium Chip Material. The chest drop rates: Tank Epic (7.08% each), Aircraft Epic (7.08% each), Missile Epic (7.08% each), Tank Legendary (1.25% each), Aircraft Legendary (1.25% each), Missile Legendary (1.25% each).

### Premium Chip Material — the crafting bottleneck

Each Legendary chip costs 800 Premium Chip Material to craft. A full set of 4 Legendary chips for one squad requires 3,200 Premium Chip Material. This is THE primary bottleneck for chip progression.

**Daily sources (F2P):** Versus Mode milestones (up to 30 daily — highest priority), Campaign Store exchanges (15–25 materials), Alliance Exercise participation (8–15 materials), Ghost Ops completion (10–20 materials). Total F2P daily: roughly 60–90 materials, meaning one Legendary chip takes approximately 9–13 days of consistent farming.

**Additional sources:** Skill Chip Chests (100 Premium Material per Epic/Legendary chest), special events, Alliance Duel rewards (3rd, 6th, 9th boxes), Engineer profession skill bonuses. Premium Chip Material CANNOT be converted from Basic Chip Material — they are completely separate resources.

**Basic Chip Material** is used to craft Epic chips (400 per chip, except Initial Move which costs 800). Basic Material primarily comes from Campaign Store and is scarcer than Premium Material despite being lower value. Epic chips crafted from Basic Material cannot be merged into Legendary chips. Their main late-game role is as Combat Boost fuel after your squads have Legendary chips equipped.

### Drone skins — cosmetic with minor bonuses

Drone skins provide small stat bonuses (+ATK, +crit rate) but are overwhelmingly cosmetic. The bonuses are tiny compared to what you get from Parts, Components, and Legendary Chips. Skins unlock at drone levels 1, 50, 100, 150, 200, and 250. Participate in seasonal events if you like the look, but never treat skins as a power investment.

## Conclusion: the principles that matter most

The entire game reduces to a handful of compounding principles. **Concentration beats diversification** — one maxed squad, one researched troop type, one gear set at full stars outperforms scattered investments every time. **Events drive progression** — 60–80% of meaningful materials come from strategic event participation, making the weekly Alliance Duel cycle the backbone of serious play. **Speedup discipline** — using speedups only during scoring windows effectively doubles their value and is the single easiest habit that separates fast-progressing accounts from stalled ones. **Type advantage is king** — the 44% effective power swing from correct counter-matching, combined with the 20% mono-type bonus, means combat math matters far more than raw power numbers. And **alliance coordination beats individual strength** — a well-organized 100M power alliance consistently defeats uncoordinated 500M power alliances in every community account of server wars. **The Drone system is the hidden multiplier** — proper Skill Chip optimization with type-matched Legendary chips and Combat Boost milestones adds 60–150% effective combat power that most players leave on the table.

The meta continues evolving with each season, but these structural principles remain stable. Tank dominance gives way to Aircraft around Day 70, Missile squads serve as counter-picks, and Season 4+ Tactics Cards introduce hybrid squad flexibility. The players who thrive long-term are not necessarily the biggest spenders — they are the ones who understand the math, respect the compounding nature of daily consistency, and coordinate effectively with their alliance.
