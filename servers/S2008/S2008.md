## Key Observations

### TwTp Dominance is Total

TwTp leads in every measurable dimension: #1 alliance power (4.54B, 16% gap over #2), 46 players in hero power top 200, 10 of top 20 hero power slots, 7 of 9 HQ 30 players, and 33 of top 100 HQ levels. This is not just a leading alliance — it is a server-defining force. Their infrastructure lead (7 HQ 30s vs 1 each for DXCI and Mazu) means they likely have the most T10 troop access on the server.

### Alliance Power Structure

The server has a clear hierarchy without an obvious formal coalition structure (unlike S2011's SNW+MHF+MAZ1):

**Tier 1 — TwTp** (4.54B) stands alone with a 623M gap over #2.

**Tier 2 — DXCI** (3.92B), **VNGO** (3.43B), **DRAX** (3.37B) form the competitive core. Any two of these combined would challenge TwTp. DXCI has the highest average hero power (30.1M) among alliances with 10+ players — their top players individually outperform TwTp's on average, but TwTp wins on sheer volume.

**Tier 3 — OSAS** (3.12B), **Ksw** (2.75B), **Sea8** (2.61B), **JHTX** (2.60B) are strong enough to be kingmakers in any server-wide conflict.

**Mazu** (2.21B, rank #14) is notably the weakest alliance with an HQ 30 player and the #1 hero power player — CColin's 58.5M hero power makes them individually the most dangerous player on the server, but the alliance lacks the depth to match its top-end quality.

### Server Demographics

Two primary player communities:
- **Chinese/Taiwanese**: TwTp, DXCI, Mazu, JHTX, 9662 — ~45%+ of top 200
- **Vietnamese**: DRAX, VNGO, Ksw, 1307, Sea8 — ~35%+ of top 200
- **International/Mixed**: OSAS, DEIN, MVDC, SIVs, OgOg — remainder

This cultural split is an important political dynamic — cross-language coalitions face communication challenges that mono-language groups don't.

### Comparison to S2011

S2008 is structurally different from S2011. Where S2011 has a formal 3-alliance coalition (SNW+MHF+MAZ1 at ~14B combined), S2008 has a single dominant alliance (TwTp at 4.54B) with no visible formal coalition. S2008's power distribution is more top-heavy — TwTp alone exceeds any single S2011 alliance. In a hypothetical CSW matchup, TwTp's HQ 30 depth (7 players) and hero power concentration would be the primary concern.
